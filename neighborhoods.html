Where to Stay in Dallas

Dallas isn't one experience — it's six or seven. Pick the neighborhood that matches your trip, then compare hotel prices in that area.

This guide covers the main hotel-dense neighborhoods in Dallas, TX, what each is known for, and what kind of trip each one is best for. Click any "See hotels" button to compare live prices on Booking.com filtered to that area.

Downtown Dallas

The convention and arts core. Kay Bailey Hutchison Convention Center, AT&T Performing Arts Center, Pioneer Plaza, Dealey Plaza, and the Sixth Floor Museum all sit within a 10-block radius. Walkable to the West End and Klyde Warren Park.

Best for: Convention attendees, sightseers, business travelers

See hotels in Downtown Dallas

Uptown

McKinney Avenue runs through it — a walkable strip of restaurants, bars, and the M-Line trolley (free). Klyde Warren Park bridges Uptown to downtown. Heavy on patio dining and well-lit at night.

Best for: Couples, foodies, leisure travelers

See hotels in Uptown

Deep Ellum

Live music venues, street art, and bars east of downtown. Original blues and jazz district from the 1920s, now a mix of dive bars, breweries, and indie galleries. Loud at night, quieter weekday afternoons.

Best for: Nightlife, music fans, younger travelers

See hotels in Deep Ellum

Bishop Arts District (Oak Cliff)

A walkable few blocks of independent boutiques, bakeries, and restaurants in north Oak Cliff. Lower density than the urban core, more local feel. Hattie's and Eno's Pizza Tavern are anchors.

Best for: Weekend trips, boutique shoppers, design-conscious travelers

See hotels in Bishop Arts District (Oak Cliff)

Las Colinas / Irving

15 minutes from DFW airport, 20 from downtown Dallas. Corporate campuses (ExxonMobil, McKesson) and the Toyota Music Factory entertainment district. Best base for early flights or business meetings on the west side of the metroplex.

Best for: Business travelers, airport stays

See hotels in Las Colinas / Irving

Highland Park / University Park

Affluent residential enclave around SMU. Highland Park Village (one of the oldest shopping centers in the country) and tree-lined streets. Quieter, no nightlife to speak of — but easy access to downtown via N Central Expressway.

Best for: Luxury, families, SMU visits
